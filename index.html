About Me

Hello World! My name is Emanuel Redondo but everyone calls me EJ. I am excited to continue my career of professional programming and hope you will take a look into my adventures. I mainly focus on graphics and tools programming and I am looking forward to one day working as a professional graphics programmer. I love computer graphics because it combines two of my favorite things - art and computers! I am also open to positions as a tools programmer because it will expose me to many different problems and people in the industry. If you like what you see, be sure to contact me. You may also download my resume from this site as well.

Work Experience

I spent a year working as a contracted Jr. Graphics Programmer at San Diego Studio who is best known for their hit series, MLB The Show. I specifcally worked on MLB The Show 23 until its release on March 23, 2023. After that, I continued to fulfill my role for the Studio's next title. As a Jr. Graphics Programmer I mainly worked in tools, supporting artist and graphics programmers during the development of our games. A big part of my job was to help ease the workload on my team members. There was significant techincal debt that had built up over the years during development and I was tasked with helping break down that debt alongside my team. as a result, my tasks stretched over a wide range of contributions in UI, utilities, automation, serliazation/deserialization, and graphics. I performed my duties in a fast paced agile environment maintaining clear and constant communication with my team members. Furthermore I made use of my extensive experience in C++ and Python programming as well as some bash scripting while working with a large AAA engine. One tool I have become very familiar with and has proved incredibly useful throughout both my professional and personal endeavors is ImGui, a graphical user interface. Although I can't go into detail about how I used it during my contract I hope you can take a look at my Real-Time ImGui OpenGL Mesh Viewer. This is a personal project I worked on last year just before getting my job at San Diego Studio.
